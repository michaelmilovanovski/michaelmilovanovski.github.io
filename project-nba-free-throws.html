Basketball Analytics

NBA Free-Throw Performance Analysis

Role: End-to-end data analyst  •  Tools: SQL (Oracle), Tableau, Excel

Overview

Free throws are one of the most efficient scoring opportunities in basketball, but not all teams and players convert them equally. This project analyzes NBA free-throw performance across multiple seasons, focusing on:

  • Which teams generate the most free-throw attempts
  • Which players consistently convert at a high percentage
  • How free-throw efficiency relates to overall scoring

Dataset & Tools

  • Play-by-play / box score data from multiple NBA seasons
  • Loaded into an Oracle database for querying with SQL
  • Visualizations built in Tableau, exported as PNGs for the portfolio

Process

  1. Imported and cleaned raw game and player statistics.
  2. Created aggregated tables at player-season and team-season levels.
  3. Calculated free-throw attempts per game and free-throw percentage.
  4. Identified top performers and trends across seasons.
  5. Designed visualizations to highlight key comparisons.
-- Example: top free-throw shooters by efficiency (min volume filter)
SELECT
    player_name,
    season,
    total_fta,
    total_ftm,
    ROUND(total_ftm / NULLIF(total_fta, 0), 3) AS ft_percentage
FROM player_season_ft
WHERE total_fta >= 200
ORDER BY ft_percentage DESC;

Key Insights

  • A small group of high-usage players account for a large share of total free-throw attempts, especially on teams built around driving guards or primary scorers.
  • Teams that draw a high volume of free throws but convert at below-average percentages leave significant points on the table over a season.
  • Several role players demonstrate elite free-throw efficiency, providing hidden value in late-game situations.
