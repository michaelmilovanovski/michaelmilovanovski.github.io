Machine Learning

Customer Churn Prediction

Level: Predictive Modeling • Tools: Python, scikit-learn, XGBoost • Role: Data scientist & analyst

Overview

This project builds a machine learning system that predicts which telecom customers are likely to cancel their service (churn). By identifying at-risk customers before they leave, companies can implement targeted retention strategies.

The project follows the complete data science workflow: exploratory analysis, data preprocessing, model training, evaluation, and business insight generation.

The Problem: 26.5% Churn Rate (Interactive)

Roughly 1 in 4 customers left the service. This represents significant revenue loss and highlights the need for proactive retention strategies.

Model Performance (Interactive)

Three models were trained and compared. Logistic Regression achieved the best overall performance:

  • ROC-AUC: 0.842 (strong discrimination ability)
  • Accuracy: 80.7%
  • Precision: 66.0%
  • Recall: 56.1% (identifies majority of churners)

ROC Curves (Interactive)

All three models show strong discrimination ability with AUC scores above 0.83, significantly better than random classification.

What Drives Churn? (Interactive)

The model identified contract type and internet service as the strongest predictors of churn, followed by tenure and payment method.

Contract Type is Critical (Interactive)

Month-to-month customers churn at 42.7% compared to just 2.8% for two-year contracts — a 15x difference.

Key Insights

  • Contract type is critical: Month-to-month customers churn at significantly higher rates than those on 1-2 year contracts.
  • New customers are high-risk: Most churn occurs within the first 6 months of service.
  • Fiber optic shows higher churn: Customers with fiber optic internet leave more often, suggesting potential service quality issues.
  • Payment method matters: Electronic check users churn more frequently than automatic payment users.
  • Support services help retention: Customers with OnlineSecurity and TechSupport services churn less.

Business Recommendations

  • Target new customers with retention offers during the first 6 months
  • Incentivize longer contracts with discounts for 1-2 year commitments
  • Investigate fiber optic service quality and customer satisfaction
  • Encourage automatic payment enrollment to reduce churn risk
  • Bundle security and support features to increase customer stickiness

Business Impact

Using this model on 1,409 test customers, we identified 210 of 374 churners (56% recall). At an average customer value of $500/year, this represents $52,500 in potential savings assuming a 50% retention rate through targeted intervention.

Dataset

The Telco Customer Churn dataset contains information about telecom customers and whether they churned. Each record includes demographic information, account details, services subscribed, and billing information.

  • Source: Kaggle — Telco Customer Churn
  • Records: 7,043 customers
  • Features: 21 (demographics, services, billing, contract)
  • Target: Churn (Yes/No) — 26.5% churn rate

Methodology

  • Exploratory Analysis: Analyzed churn patterns, identified missing values, visualized feature relationships
  • Preprocessing: Handled missing data, encoded categorical variables (one-hot encoding), scaled numerical features
  • Train/Test Split: 80/20 stratified split to maintain churn ratio
  • Models Trained: Logistic Regression, Random Forest, XGBoost
  • Evaluation: Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1-Score, ROC-AUC, confusion matrices
